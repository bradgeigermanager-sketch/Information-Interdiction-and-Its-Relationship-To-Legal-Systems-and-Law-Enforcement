# 📘 **CONSOLIDATED FEDERAL ARREST PROCEDURES MANUAL**  
*(Synthesized from DOJ, FBI, DHS, ATF, USMS, and FLETC doctrine)*

---

# **SECTION I — LEGAL FOUNDATIONS**

## **1. Constitutional Authority**
All federal arrests must comply with:

- **Fourth Amendment** — no unreasonable searches or seizures  
- **Fifth Amendment** — self‑incrimination protections (Miranda)  
- **Sixth Amendment** — right to counsel  
- **Fourteenth Amendment** — due process and equal protection  

**Core DOJ Principle:**  
Arrests must be objectively reasonable, supported by lawful authority, and executed with respect for civil rights.

---

## **2. Probable Cause Standard**
Across all agencies (DOJ, FBI, DHS, ATF, USMS):

- Probable cause exists when facts would lead a reasonable officer to believe a crime has been committed.
- Hunches, intuition, or uncorroborated tips are insufficient.
- Probable cause must be articulable and documented.

**DOJ CRM:** Probable cause is the “constitutional threshold” for any arrest.

---

## **3. Arrest Warrants**
### **When Required**
- Entry into a private residence  
- Arrests for non‑felony offenses outside officer presence  
- Situations lacking exigent circumstances  

### **Exceptions (all agencies)**
- Exigent circumstances  
- Hot pursuit  
- Public‑place felony arrests  
- Immediate threat to life or evidence  

**USMS & FBI:** Warrant service must include knock‑and‑announce unless exception applies.

---

# **SECTION II — OPERATIONAL PROCEDURES**

## **4. Pre‑Arrest Planning**
Common across FBI, ATF, USMS, DHS:

- Confirm identity of suspect  
- Verify warrant validity (if applicable)  
- Conduct risk assessment (weapons, associates, environment)  
- Assign roles (contact officer, cover officer, arresting officer)  
- Establish communications plan  
- Coordinate with local agencies if needed  

**FLETC doctrine:** Planning reduces force likelihood and increases officer safety.

---

## **5. Approach & Contact**
### **General Rules**
- Use the least confrontational approach consistent with safety  
- Maintain situational awareness  
- Identify yourself as law enforcement  
- Provide clear, lawful commands  

**CBP & ICE:** Officers must display credentials unless unsafe.

---

## **6. Use‑of‑Force Standards**
All federal agencies follow a **force continuum**:

1. Officer presence  
2. Verbal commands  
3. Soft control techniques  
4. Hard control techniques  
5. Intermediate weapons  
6. Deadly force  

**DOJ Deadly Force Policy (applies to FBI, DEA, ATF, USMS):**  
Deadly force only when necessary to prevent imminent death or serious physical injury.

**CBP:** Must consider totality of circumstances and avoid creating unnecessary jeopardy.

---

## **7. Arrest Execution**
### **Steps**
- Communicate intent to arrest  
- Control hands immediately  
- Apply restraints properly  
- Search incident to arrest (within legal limits)  
- Monitor subject for medical distress  

**FLETC:** Handcuffing must be double‑locked and checked for fit.

---

## **8. Miranda Requirements**
Miranda applies when BOTH conditions are met:

- **Custody**  
- **Interrogation**

Officers must:

- Deliver the warning clearly  
- Ensure comprehension  
- Document invocation or waiver  

**FBI DIOG:** Agents must stop questioning immediately if rights are invoked.

---

# **SECTION III — SPECIALIZED FEDERAL GUIDANCE**

## **9. High‑Risk Arrests**
(USMS, FBI, ATF)

- Use tactical teams when risk is elevated  
- Conduct pre‑raid briefing  
- Use less‑lethal options when feasible  
- Prioritize safety of bystanders  

---

## **10. Warrant Service**
(USMS lead doctrine)

- Verify address  
- Knock‑and‑announce unless exception  
- Use dynamic entry only when justified  
- Maintain perimeter security  

---

## **11. Immigration‑Related Arrests**
(ICE ERO)

- Administrative warrants differ from criminal warrants  
- Arrests in sensitive locations require supervisory approval  
- Documentation must include alienage and removability basis  

---

## **12. Border‑Area Arrests**
(CBP)

- Officers have extended authority within 100 miles of border  
- Still must meet constitutional standards  
- Use‑of‑force policy identical to DOJ deadly‑force rules  

---

# **SECTION IV — POST‑ARREST PROCEDURES**

## **13. Transport & Custody**
All agencies require:

- Search of vehicle before and after transport  
- Seat‑belt use for detainee  
- Constant monitoring  
- Immediate medical attention if needed  

---

## **14. Booking & Documentation**
- Document probable cause  
- Record Miranda administration  
- Log evidence  
- Notify prosecutors  
- Complete arrest report promptly  

**DOJ JM:** Accuracy and completeness are essential for admissibility.

---

# **SECTION V — CIVIL RIGHTS & ACCOUNTABILITY**

## **15. Prohibited Conduct**
- Racial profiling  
- Excessive force  
- Coercive interrogation  
- Retaliatory arrest  
- Fabrication of evidence  

**COPS Office:** Agencies must maintain bias‑free policing policies.

---

## **16. Required Reporting**
- Use‑of‑force reports  
- Injuries to officers or subjects  
- Property damage  
- Civil rights complaints  

---

# **SECTION VI — TRAINING & PROFESSIONAL STANDARDS**

## **17. Mandatory Training Areas**
(FLETC baseline + DOJ requirements)

- Constitutional law  
- Arrest & control  
- De‑escalation  
- Firearms  
- Defensive tactics  
- Crisis intervention  
- Cultural competency  

---

# **SECTION VII — QUICK REFERENCE CHECKLIST**

### **Before Arrest**
- Probable cause established  
- Warrant verified (if needed)  
- Risk assessment completed  

### **During Arrest**
- Identify as law enforcement  
- Use minimal necessary force  
- Control hands  
- Restrain safely  

### **After Arrest**
- Miranda (if interrogating)  
- Search incident to arrest  
- Transport safely  
- Document everything
